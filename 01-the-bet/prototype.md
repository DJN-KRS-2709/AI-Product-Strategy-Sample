# The Prototype Bet

## What I Built

A clickable prototype of a renewal-risk cockpit that ranks at-risk accounts, explains the top drivers, and drafts a 30-day save plan for CSM approval.

The prototype demonstrates three moments:

1. A VP of Customer Success opens the portfolio view and sees the top 10 accounts most likely to churn.
2. A CSM opens one account and sees evidence-backed risk drivers: usage drop, unresolved support tickets, executive sponsor change, and negative call sentiment.
3. The CSM generates a save plan, edits it, and approves the next-best actions back into Salesforce.

## Tool Used

Cursor for the prototype shell, mock account data, and interaction logic. Figma was used for the initial dashboard sketch.

## Prototype Link

`https://renewalradar.example/demo`

## AI Value Archetype

**Copilot + Oracle**

RenewalRadar is an Oracle when it predicts which accounts are at risk and why. It becomes a Copilot when it drafts the account-specific save plan and lets the CSM edit, approve, or reject it.

## The Bet in One Sentence

Customer Success teams will pay for an AI renewal copilot that converts scattered customer signals into evidence-backed save plans because renewal risk is rising, CSM teams are overloaded, and existing health scores are too shallow to drive action.

## Kill Criteria

Stop or materially pivot if any of these are true after the first 90-day pilot:

- Fewer than 60% of flagged high-risk accounts are judged useful by CSMs.
- AI-flagged accounts do not show at least an 8 percentage point lift in save-rate versus matched control accounts.
- CSMs reject or heavily rewrite more than 50% of generated save plans.
- Model and infrastructure costs push AI-adjusted gross margin below 55% at normal usage.
